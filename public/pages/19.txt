see all my reviews angels and insects is one of the most visually stunning movies i have ever seen but every bit of the immense beauty &amp; luxury that is on the screen contributes to the sense &amp; definition of exquisite excess that is so ingrained in the alabasters' way of life. if one imagines this movie as a 1940s hollywood effort, perhaps starring olivier &amp; fontaine, ala rebecca, then one must really appreciate the subtly shaded performances. i have never seen better acting onscreen, especially in the performances of rylance &amp; thomas. and all the interplay of idea &amp; emotion, science &amp; passion, objectivity &amp; subjectivity, so rarely transferable from print to film are so brilliantly achieved here. hey, even the sex scenes are among the best i've ever viewed. 9 of 11 people found this review helpful.&nbsp;
