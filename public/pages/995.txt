frequently asked questions this faq is empty. add the first question. user reviews even funnier with repeated viewing 13 march 2002 | by movie-robot (movies@shtick.org) &ndash; see all my reviews without exaggeration, i can tell you that i've seen this movie at least 30 times. and i always find something new about it. for instance, in ron &amp; sheila's audition with their treatment of &quot;midnight at the oasis&quot;, it took me about 10 viewings til i noticed that sheila is mouthing the words to ron because of his problems remembering his lines.i really don't know where to begin listing my favorite things about this movie - ron's &quot;medical reason&quot; for his sole trip outside of blaine, dr. pearl's carson impressions, sheila's &quot;less-is-more&quot; acting approach, corky's tantrums (&quot;i hate you, and i hate your ass face!&quot;), libba mae's description of her job at dairy queen, councilman steve stark admiration for corky (&quot;god, i wish i was in the show&quot;), there is not a wasted moment in this film. it's stupid of me to try to list them here.the extras on the dvd feature a ton of scenes that weren't in the movie. there's some additional corky items in his memorabilia collection, including towels from &quot;hamlet on ice&quot;, alternate epilogues for both ron &amp; sheila and also dr. pearl, a scene of corky driving around town telling people they made the cast, a nutty scene of ron's whiffle-ball reenactment of bill mazeroski's famous home-run, dinner at johnny savage's house, and extra stuff from the musical - &quot;nothing ever happens in blaine&quot;, &quot;this bulging river&quot; and also a whacked-out white house scene. but the piece de resistance of the deleted scenes is libby mae's other audition piece which is so subtly twisted that you just need to see it yourself.it's debatable whether this movie is outright cruel in making fun of small townsfolk &amp; community theater types. but the more i watch it and get into it, i think that everybody in the movie has a strong amount of affection for their characters. i don't know; you watch it 30 times and tell me what you think. 11 of 13 people found this review helpful.&nbsp;
