i&#39;m tigger! t-i-double ger. that spells tigger! 28 july 2007 | by atreyu_ii (the world of artists) &ndash; see all my reviews &quot;winnie the pooh and the blustery day&quot;, the second of the winnie the pooh&#39;s shorts, introduces us the characters that were missing in the first one: piglet (who becomes pooh&#39;s best friend) and the very humorous and super-amusing tigger.it has all of the qualities already seen in the first one, including cheerful songs such as &quot;the wonderful thing about tiggers&quot;, &quot;hip hip pooh-ray!&quot; and &quot;the rain rain rain came down down down&quot;.this one has a funny plot as well. in the first part of the story, winnie the pooh wishes a &laquo;happy winds-day&raquo; to his friends (because it&#39;s a very windy day), starting with piglet. the second part of the story takes place later, while pooh&#39;s in bed and hears strange noises and decides to open the door anyway (after all, he&#39;s not the smartest bear in the world). this visit is... tigger! tigger is so funny! i love his remarkable personality, his sense of humor, his singing and his bounces! it&#39;s impossible not to like tigger! during the third part of our story, there&#39;s a rain storm and a hero party that becomes a two hero party, celebrated by cristopher robin and his pals. the owl is a character that loves to talk. once he starts, he doesn&#39;t stop and he dislikes to be interrupted, although he is always polite. the gopher appears in this one too, but he&#39;s not in the book, you know. my favorite piglet&#39;s line is &laquo;oh, dear! oh, d-d-d-dear-dear!&raquo;.my least favorite part is pooh&#39;s dream about &quot;hefalumps and woozels&quot; trying to steal his honey. nevertheless, it&#39;s bizarre, original and creative. i can&#39;t help but think of the &quot;pink elephants&quot; sequence from &quot;dumbo&quot;, because they&#39;re both so odd.initially i had doubts about the sex of rabbit and piglet because their voices sound somewhat feminine.well, t-t-f-n!
