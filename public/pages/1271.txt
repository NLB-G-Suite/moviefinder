user reviews &nbsp; pretty good, but starts way too slow for a family film. 10 may 2014 | by geeky randy (united states) &ndash; see all my reviews 8-year-old wilson (still as sweet as when we saw her in mrs. doubtfire) wishes for her cabbie father pastorelli to win the leading role in a broadway adaptation of dickens&#x27; a tale of two cities, and short (still called a &#x22;fairy godmother&#x22; despite being a male) arrives to (attempt to) grant it to her. fans of short&#x27;s comedy will get it, and even if you&#x27;re not a fan of his work there are still some worthy moments; however, the story takes way too long to get going for a movie targeted at children. capra&#x27;s character also slows the movie down as wilson&#x27;s stereotypical punk older brother who&#x27;s protective of his younger sister but is too cool to show it. the musical numbers in the theatre scenes are a nice touch, but it&#x27;s difficult to give the movie brownie-points for it since the film itself is not a musical. jaro dick&#x27;s set decoration and luke reichle&#x27;s costume design is terrific. hey! one of the fairy godmother&#x27;s (bunty webb) was the &#x27;ay caramba&#x27;
