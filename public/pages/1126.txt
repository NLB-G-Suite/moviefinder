referred to in at least two articles i&#x27;ve read in past months dealing with the clinton/trump presidential race. now, having seen it i wonder why it was mentioned at all. an incredibly superficial ninety-six minutes of film clips strung together with no commentary or on-screen captions to reveal dates, locations or personalities. plenty of shots of carville on the phone but as the person he&#x27;s speaking to isn&#x27;t heard it&#x27;s a bit like reading only the left-hand pages of a novel because all the right-hand pages are blank. lots of clips of people talking to each other but mostly breezy chit-chat and laughter with no real substance to help the viewer understand what was going on.no doubt this visual stream of consciousness style of documentary can deliver a coherent movie on any subject but it would take a director&#x27;s skill and planning to make it work, if the &#x27;war room&#x27; is the best of the hours and hours of shot material i reckon it&#x27;s a dismal failure and i doubt many of the folk who sat through it all will see it twice. 0 of 0 people found this review helpful.&nbsp;
