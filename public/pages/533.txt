which deals with common and uncommon themes, such as home, sexual identity and love. van sant throws in some shakespearean language plagiarised straight from henry iv and a non-linear narrative and you&#39;ve got one very cute surrealist indie film.the credits to this film are it&#39;s director and star, river phoenix, whose understated and moving performance lifts this film above the trash it easily could&#39;ve been in another director or actor&#39;s hands. van sant uses symbols to represent emotional states and his use of special effects is limited and effective. there is some really heartfelt dialogue in this movie, especially the much-mentioned camp-fire scene.this film can be irritating; sometimes the shakespearean dialogue doesn&#39;t work (and it&#39;s okay to admit that a near-perfect film like this has flaws) and reeves is sometimes a little stiff. the film is mostly redeemed by its bitter-sweet ending and fun opening titles.i&#39;m not going to tackle any issues in this film because i just don&#39;t have the energy. just appreciate the film for what it is and have a nice day. :) 33 of 44 people found this review helpful.&nbsp;
