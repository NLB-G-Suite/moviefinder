the forgotten prelude to world war 2 30 may 2002 | by jay harris (sirbossman6969@yahoo.com) (united states) &ndash; see all my reviews ken loach proves once again, that he is a director of the very highest calibre. he has crafted a film about the spanish civil war (1936-9), which was the prelude to ww2. truthfully i could care less about the politics of this tragic episode in world history, but the director,by his sheer genius &amp; craftsmanship made me sit at the edge of my seat &amp; pay close attention. subtitles are used sparingly &amp; it is hard to make some of the dialogue out due thick accent, the acting is so skillfull you needed no dialogue,you are able to understand each actors feelings &amp; emotions. credit this to mr.loach, the only actor i have heard of in this film is the lead ian hart/ he &amp; all the others do an excellent job. this film was made in 1995, &amp; barely released,another example of distributors not knowing a great film from a hole in the ground. even this is a war film, there is very little violence, a good history lesson for the younger ones, then a trip to the library to find out more.my rating is ***1/2 95/100 points 9 on imdb 15 of 20 people found this review helpful.&nbsp;
